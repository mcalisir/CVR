What is S-CVI/Ave?

Scale-level CVI Average: most commonly used scale-level validity indicator

S-CVI/Ave (Scale-level Content Validity Index/Average) is the most reliable and widely used measure of scale-level content validity. It represents the average of all item-level I-CVI values across the entire scale.

S-CVI/Ave Formula

S-CVI/Ave = Sum of all I-CVI values / Number of items

Validity Threshold

S-CVI/Ave ≥ 0.90: Excellent content validity ✓

Scale is ready for use without further modifications.

S-CVI/Ave < 0.90: Insufficient validity

Items require revision or removal.

Why S-CVI/Ave is Preferred

  • Captures item-level expert agreement more precisely than CVI
  • Most widely cited in modern research literature
  • Clear, single threshold (0.90) for excellence
  • Recommended by Waltz, Strickland, and Lenz (2005)

References

Waltz, C. F., Strickland, O. L., & Lenz, E. R. (2005). Measurement in nursing and health research. Springer Publishing Company.
