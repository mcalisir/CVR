What is S-CVI/Ave?

Scale-level CVI Average: most reliable and widely recommended scale validity measure

S-CVI/Ave (Scale-level Content Validity Index/Average) is the most reliable and widely recommended measure of scale-level content validity in modern research. It represents the average of all item-level I-CVI values across an entire scale or instrument. This metric has become the gold standard in scale development because it combines the benefits of item-level analysis with a comprehensive scale summary.

S-CVI/Ave is used in:

  • Nursing and healthcare scale development
  • Educational assessment instrument validation
  • Psychological and behavioral measurement tools
  • Organizational and management research
  • Any field requiring expert-based instrument validation

S-CVI/Ave Formula

S-CVI/Ave = Sum of all I-CVI values / Number of items

How to Calculate

Step 1: Calculate I-CVI for each item (using the I-CVI formula: experts rating as essential / total experts)

Step 2: Add all I-CVI values together

Step 3: Divide by the total number of items

Example

A 5-item scale with these I-CVI values (5 experts rated each item):
Item 1: I-CVI = 1.0 (5/5 experts agreed)
Item 2: I-CVI = 0.8 (4/5 experts agreed)
Item 3: I-CVI = 0.8 (4/5 experts agreed)
Item 4: I-CVI = 1.0 (5/5 experts agreed)
Item 5: I-CVI = 0.6 (3/5 experts agreed)

S-CVI/Ave = (1.0 + 0.8 + 0.8 + 1.0 + 0.6) / 5 = 4.2 / 5 = 0.84

Validity Threshold

S-CVI/Ave ≥ 0.90: Excellent content validity ✓

Scale is ready for use. Proceed to reliability testing and empirical validation.

S-CVI/Ave 0.80–0.89: Acceptable validity (some revision recommended)

Items with lower I-CVI should be revised or removed.

S-CVI/Ave < 0.80: Insufficient validity

Scale requires substantial revision. Conduct another expert review after making changes.

Why S-CVI/Ave is Preferred

  • Captures precision: Uses I-CVI values which are easier to interpret than CVR
  • Industry standard: Most widely cited in published research (especially nursing, education, psychology)
  • Clear threshold: Simple, round number (0.90) that's easy to communicate
  • Recommended by experts: Endorsed by Waltz, Strickland, Lenz, Polit, and Beck
  • Balances rigor and practicality: More stringent than CVI but achievable with good items
  • Item-level visibility: You can see which items contributed to the overall score

Practical Guidance

If S-CVI/Ave = 0.95

✓ Excellent! Your scale has outstanding content validity. Proceed directly to pilot testing and reliability analysis.

If S-CVI/Ave = 0.88

⚠ Good, but could be better. Review items with I-CVI < 0.80, consider revising them, and optionally conduct another expert review round.

If S-CVI/Ave = 0.75

✗ Needs work. Remove or substantially revise items with I-CVI < 0.70, then conduct another expert panel review before proceeding.

Reporting S-CVI/Ave

How to report in your research paper:

"The S-CVI/Ave was 0.92, indicating excellent content validity. Of the 20 items, 18 achieved an I-CVI of 0.78 or higher, while 2 items were revised based on expert feedback and excluded."

Combining S-CVI/Ave with S-CVI/UA

Best practice is to report both metrics:

  • S-CVI/Ave ≥ 0.90: Shows strong average agreement
  • S-CVI/UA ≥ 0.80: Shows most items had unanimous expert agreement

Interpretation: An S-CVI/Ave of 0.92 and S-CVI/UA of 0.85 means experts largely agreed items were essential, and 85% of items had perfect expert consensus.
