What is I-CVI?

Item-level Content Validity Index: measures expert agreement on individual item relevance

I-CVI (Item-level Content Validity Index) is a validity metric that measures the proportion of experts who agree that a specific item on a measurement instrument is relevant and should be retained. Unlike CVR, I-CVI provides a straightforward probability-based measure ranging from 0 to 1.

I-CVI is particularly useful because it:

  • Is easier to interpret than CVR (ranges from 0 to 1)
  • Directly represents the proportion of expert agreement
  • Has well-established evaluation criteria from multiple sources
  • Works well with both small and large expert panels

I-CVI Formula

I-CVI = Number rating item as essential / Total number of experts

Interpretation

I-CVI directly represents the proportion of experts who rated the item as essential:

  • I-CVI = 1.0: 100% of experts agree item is essential
  • I-CVI = 0.8: 80% of experts agree item is essential
  • I-CVI = 0.5: 50% of experts agree (no consensus)
  • I-CVI = 0.0: 0% of experts agree (should definitely be removed)

Acceptable I-CVI Thresholds

Different evaluation criteria apply depending on the number of experts on your panel. The most commonly cited standards are from Lynn (1986) and Polit & Beck (2006):

ExpertsCriterionStatusAction
1-2I-CVI = 1.0Valid ✓Retain item
3+I-CVI ≥ 0.78Valid ✓Retain item
3+0.70 to 0.78QuestionableRevise or gather more data
3+I-CVI < 0.70InvalidRemove or substantially revise

Why These Thresholds?

With 1-2 experts, you need perfect agreement (1.0) because the expert panel is so small. With 3+ experts, 0.78 is considered the minimum threshold because this represents statistically significant agreement. The research shows that items rated essential by 78% of experts are consistently validated in later testing.

Practical Example

Example: Nursing competency scale

Situation: A nursing board develops a competency assessment with 5 experts reviewing a patient care item.

Ratings received:

  • Expert 1: Essential
  • Expert 2: Essential
  • Expert 3: Useful but not essential
  • Expert 4: Essential
  • Expert 5: Essential

Calculation:

I-CVI = 4 / 5 = 0.80 ✓ Valid

Interpretation: With 5 experts and minimum threshold 0.78, this item's I-CVI of 0.80 is valid. The item should be retained.

Note: I-CVI is increasingly preferred in modern research because it's easier to interpret and explain to stakeholders. Many researchers now report both I-CVI and CVR for transparency.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to adjust threshold: Don't use 0.78 with 1-2 experts — use 1.0 instead
  • Mixing rating systems: Ensure all experts use the same rating scale (1-3 or yes/no)
  • Treating "Useful" as "Essential": Only count "Essential" ratings in the numerator
  • Small expert panel: Using fewer than 3-5 experts reduces statistical power

References
