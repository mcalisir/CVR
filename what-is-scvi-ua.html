What is S-CVI/UA?

Scale-level CVI Universal Agreement: strict consensus-based validity measure

S-CVI/UA (Scale-level Content Validity Index/Universal Agreement) measures the proportion of items on which all experts unanimously agreed the item is essential. It is a more stringent validity criterion than S-CVI/Ave, requiring 100% expert consensus rather than just a high average.

S-CVI/UA is particularly useful when:

  • You need a high-stakes instrument (certification exams, safety-critical assessments)
  • Expert consensus is critically important
  • You want to complement S-CVI/Ave with a stricter measure
  • You're developing instruments for sensitive domains

S-CVI/UA Formula

S-CVI/UA = Items with I-CVI of 1.0 / Total number of items

In Plain English

S-CVI/UA is simply the percentage of items that were rated "essential" by every single expert on your panel, with no exceptions.

Example Calculation

A 10-item scale with expert ratings (5 experts per item):

Items with unanimous agreement (I-CVI = 1.0): 1, 2, 4, 7, 8
Items WITHOUT unanimous agreement (I-CVI < 1.0): 3, 5, 6, 9, 10

S-CVI/UA = 5 unanimous items / 10 total items = 0.50 or 50%

Validity Threshold

S-CVI/UA ≥ 0.80: Excellent validity ✓

At least 80% of items achieved perfect expert consensus. This is considered excellent.

S-CVI/UA 0.60–0.79: Acceptable, but not excellent

Some items lack unanimous agreement. Consider revising items with lower I-CVI values.

S-CVI/UA < 0.60: Needs improvement

More than 40% of items lack unanimous agreement. Revision needed.

S-CVI/Ave vs S-CVI/UA

These two metrics measure different things and are often reported together:

MetricDefinitionThresholdFocusStringency
S-CVI/AveAverage of all I-CVI values≥ 0.90Overall item agreementModerate
S-CVI/UA% of items with I-CVI = 1.0≥ 0.80Unanimous agreementStrict

What They Reveal Together

Scenario 1: S-CVI/Ave = 0.92, S-CVI/UA = 0.85

✓ Excellent! Experts generally agreed items were essential, AND 85% of items had perfect consensus. This is ideal.

Scenario 2: S-CVI/Ave = 0.90, S-CVI/UA = 0.50

⚠ Good average agreement, but experts disagreed on half the items. Those low-consensus items should be revised.

Scenario 3: S-CVI/Ave = 0.88, S-CVI/UA = 0.90

✓ Surprising but positive: Nearly all items had unanimous agreement, but a few low-agreement items dragged down the average. Consider removing those few items for a higher S-CVI/Ave.

When to Use S-CVI/UA vs S-CVI/Ave

Use S-CVI/Ave When:

  • You want the most commonly accepted validity measure
  • Your scale can tolerate some items with less-than-perfect consensus
  • You're following modern research standards
  • You need a 0.90 threshold that's easier to achieve

Use S-CVI/UA When:

  • You need exceptional, high-stakes validity (certification, safety)
  • Consensus is paramount
  • You want to identify which items have universal support
  • You're supplementing S-CVI/Ave for added rigor

Practical Interpretation

If S-CVI/UA = 0.90

✓ Excellent! 90% of your items have perfect expert consensus. Your scale is ready for empirical testing. This is a strong indicator of content validity.

If S-CVI/UA = 0.75

⚠ Acceptable but not excellent. 75% of items have perfect consensus, but 25% need refinement. Review items with I-CVI < 1.0 and consider revisions.

If S-CVI/UA = 0.45

✗ Too many items lack unanimous agreement. This suggests either (a) your items need better wording, (b) your expert panel wasn't well-selected, or (c) your scale needs fundamental revision.

The "Agreement Paradox"

Important insight: An item can have high I-CVI (0.85) but not achieve I-CVI of 1.0 for S-CVI/UA purposes. This means:

  • 85% of experts said "essential"
  • But 15% said "useful but not essential" or "not necessary"
  • It counts toward S-CVI/Ave but not S-CVI/UA

This is why some scales have lower S-CVI/UA but still-acceptable S-CVI/Ave — they have items with strong but not perfect consensus.

Combining Both Metrics

Best Practice: Report both S-CVI/Ave (≥ 0.90) AND S-CVI/UA (≥ 0.80) to show that your scale has:

  1. Strong average expert agreement
  2. Substantial items with perfect consensus
