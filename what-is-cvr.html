What is CVR? Content Validity Ratio Formula

Lawshe's formula, minimum thresholds, and interpretation guide for scale development

CVR (Content Validity Ratio) is a statistical measure used in scale development to evaluate how well individual items on a measurement instrument measure the intended construct. It quantifies expert panel agreement about whether each item is "essential" to include on the scale.

CVR Formula

CVR = (ne − N/2) / (N/2)

ne: Number of experts rating the item as "essential"

N: Total number of experts rating the item

Developed by C. H. Lawshe (1975), this formula produces values between −1 and +1:

  • +1: All experts agree the item is essential
  • 0: Half the experts say essential, half say not
  • −1: No experts consider it essential

Lawshe's Minimum CVR Thresholds

For an item to be considered valid, its CVR must meet or exceed the minimum threshold corresponding to the number of experts:

Number of Experts Minimum CVR Threshold
50.99
60.99
70.99
80.75
90.78
100.62
150.49
200.42

Calculation Example

Scenario: 10 experts rate an item:

  • 8 experts: "Essential" (1)
  • 2 experts: "Not essential" (3)

Calculation:

CVR = (8 − 10/2) / (10/2) = (8 − 5) / 5 = 3 / 5 = 0.60

Result: With minimum threshold 0.62, this item (0.60) falls below the cutoff and should be revised or removed.

How to Interpret CVR

  • CVR ≥ Threshold: Item is valid and can be retained
  • CVR < Threshold: Item is invalid; revision or removal is recommended
